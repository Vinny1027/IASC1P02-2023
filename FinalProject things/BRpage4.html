Aliza Howard

I'm Aliza, the journalist for BBC in this recovery project. My passion for understanding the intricate workings of the Black Rose Cult stems from a journalistic curiosity that yearns to unravel the mysteries of cults—how they form, their origins, the motivations driving their actions, and the impact they leave on individuals and society. In my journalistic pursuit within this recovery project, I've come to recognize that cults hold a unique significance within culture and society. They aren't just isolated anomalies; rather, they often mirror and magnify certain aspects of the broader social fabric. Cults, in their formation and practices, can reveal underlying societal vulnerabilities, unmet needs, or collective desires. Understanding them becomes not just an academic exercise but a crucial exploration into the intricacies of our shared cultural and societal landscape. Through shedding light on the Black Rose Cult, I aim to offer insights that extend beyond the cult itself, contributing to a greater understanding of the interplay between these groups and the societies from which they emerge.
